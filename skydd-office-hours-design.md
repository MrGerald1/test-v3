## Problem Statement

Nigerians are already saving money on apps like PiggyVest and Cowrywise — but when users engage with Skydd, they surface a deeper anxiety: **"What if I don't reach my savings goal? What if I die before then?"** The savings apps have no answer. Traditional insurance products have no savings component. The gap between "I want to save for my child's education" and "I want to guarantee that fund exists even if something happens to me" is entirely unoccupied in the Nigerian market.

Skydd v3 (BRD) proposes to fill that gap: every savings goal carries an insurance backbone that guarantees the outcome regardless of what happens to the saver.

## Demand Evidence

- Skydd already has **paying customers** on the existing insurance platform (skydd.ng), offering health, auto, travel, device, and property insurance
- During direct user observation, existing Skydd customers expressed unsolicited anxiety about savings without protection — they referenced PiggyVest specifically and asked "what if I haven't hit my goals?" and "what if I die before that?"
- This demand signal was **behavioral and unprompted** — not from a survey, not from a demo, from users expressing fear while using a product that didn't yet offer a solution
- The government (NAICOM) is mandating certain insurance classes as compulsory, creating regulatory tailwinds for insurance adoption broadly

## Status Quo

Users' current workaround:
- Save on PiggyVest or Cowrywise (no insurance, no goal guarantee)
- Buy insurance separately on Skydd or other platforms (no savings, no wealth-building component)
- These two behaviors exist in separate apps, separate mental models, and separate financial plans
- The cost of the gap: if a PiggyVest user dies 8 years into a 10-year education savings plan, their family gets whatever has been saved — not the target amount

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** Nigerian parents of young children (newborns to age 5), typically salaried professionals or dual-income households, who are already using a savings app and are beginning to think about their child's education funding. They're financially literate enough to be saving but unsophisticated enough about insurance that the concept of "guaranteed fund regardless of what happens to you" is genuinely new to them.

**Narrowest wedge:** Child Education Insurance — a savings plan with a fixed target amount, a fixed maturity date (e.g., 18 years), a calculated monthly premium, and an insurance guarantee that the full target is paid to the beneficiary even if the policyholder dies. The underwriter is revealed only on the temporary certificate. The sales and onboarding are 100% Skydd-branded.

## Constraints

- No API integration with underwriters — offline liaison model (email, portal, shared dashboard) only
- Embeddy integration (fintech wallet + virtual card) is planned but not required for the first tranche
- Existing Skydd platform (skydd.ng) has paying customers who can serve as the beta cohort — no cold start required
- Regulatory compliance with NAICOM and NDPR required
- 3–4 underwriter partnerships needed (not all at launch)

## Premises

1. The core demand signal is real: users of savings apps have a latent protection anxiety that Skydd can uniquely address by combining both in one product.
2. The offline liaison model (no API) is a feature — it removes the dependency on insurer tech readiness and allows fast onboarding of partners.
3. Child Education Insurance is the right product wedge — highest emotional stakes, clearest goal, most compelling "what if you die" story.
4. The real competitor is PiggyVest + nothing — not another insurtech. Skydd becomes the protection layer on top of savings behavior that already exists.
5. Embeddy integration is important for the long-term vision (full financial OS) but is not required for the insurance-backed savings MVP.

All premises confirmed by founder.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Insurance-Backed Savings MVP
One product (Child Education Insurance), one underwriter, reuse existing Skydd payment and onboarding rails. No admin catalogue, no multi-product engine. Ship in days.
- **Effort:** S (human: ~6–8 weeks / CC+gstack: ~1 week)
- **Risk:** Low
- **Completeness:** 5/10
- Pros: Fastest path to revenue validation; existing customers are the beta cohort
- Cons: Manual ops burden; no infrastructure for expanding to other products

### Approach B: White-Label Engine + 3 Products
Build the admin catalogue and white-label product engine. Launch Child Education, Life + Endowment, and Job Loss Insurance. No Embeddy yet.
- **Effort:** M (human: ~4 months / CC+gstack: ~3–4 weeks)
- **Risk:** Medium
- **Completeness:** 7/10
- Pros: Engine built once; each new product is config not code; covers 3 distinct demand segments
- Cons: 3 underwriter onboardings in parallel; bigger scope before savings behavior validated at scale

### Approach C: Full Platform — BRD v3 Complete
All 8 products, Embeddy wallet + virtual card, claims management, document vault, in-app notifications, policy history.
- **Effort:** XL (human: ~6–9 months / CC+gstack: ~6–8 weeks)
- **Risk:** High
- **Completeness:** 10/10
- Pros: Full super-app vision; Embeddy creates a financial OS, not just insurance; claims module creates post-sale stickiness
- Cons: Large scope before savings behavior validated; 4 underwriter partnerships simultaneously

## Recommended Approach

**Hybrid B/C — Build engine now, smart launch order.**

Build the white-label admin catalogue and certificate engine (Approach B infrastructure). Launch in two tranches:

**Tranche 1 (launch):** Child Education, Life + Endowment, Job Loss Insurance
- These three have the strongest savings-fit (★★★★★ for education and life, ★★★☆☆ for job loss)
- They cover distinct segments: parents, income earners, salaried/gig workers
- Three products validates the savings model before committing the full 8

**Tranche 2 (existing product migration):** Motor, Health, Home, Device
- Reframe the savings component as "goal fund" (e.g., "save for your next vehicle upgrade")
- These are more transactional products — the savings layer is add-on, not the core value prop
- Migrate existing Skydd customers into the new model where appropriate

**Why this works:** The engine, once built, makes adding products 4–8 near-zero marginal cost. The BRD's key insight is that the admin catalogue separates product configuration from product development. Build the factory, then run the factory.

**Critical note on product-savings fit:** Not all 8 products map cleanly to the "savings-backed" model. Device insurance (saving for the next phone upgrade) and travel insurance have weak savings fit. Don't force the framing — present them as protection products with an optional savings goal, not as savings products with insurance backing.

## Open Questions

1. **Embeddy timeline:** When does Embeddy integration become a competitive necessity? If a competitor launches savings + wallet, does the Skydd card become urgent?
2. **Underwriter for tranche 1:** Which specific underwriter has been engaged and can execute the offline liaison model for Child Education Insurance?
3. **Savings interest rate:** The BRD mentions "projected interest rate" in the savings calculator — is this a fixed return guaranteed by the underwriter, or a projected figure? This is a major regulatory and trust question.
4. **Claims volume:** The claims management module is described as forwarding to insurers offline. At what volume does this manual process break?
5. **Existing Skydd products:** Will the current health/auto/device products at skydd.ng be migrated to the new platform or run in parallel?

## Success Criteria

- Child Education Insurance: 100 paying policies in the first 60 days post-launch
- Monthly premium collection via existing Skydd payment rails (no new integration required)
- Temporary certificate issued within 60 seconds of payment
- Final underwriter certificate uploaded to app within 5 business days
- Zero failed claims in the first 6 months (claims management SLA)
- Admin catalogue configured for at least 2 underwriter products per product line before launch

## Dependencies

- At least one underwriter partner committed to the offline liaison model for Child Education Insurance
- Embeddy partnership for Tranche 2 wallet integration
- NAICOM compliance review of the white-label certificate model
- Savings calculator actuarial inputs (interest rate, mortality table) from underwriter

## The Assignment

**Before writing a single line of code:** Get on a call with your top 10 existing Skydd customers and ask them one question: "If you could have saved for your child's education through Skydd, and we guaranteed the full amount would be paid out even if something happened to you — what would you have paid per month for that?" Listen for the number. If 7 out of 10 give you a number, you have a product. If they pause and say "that's interesting, I'd have to think about it" — you don't yet.
